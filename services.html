Case study: Service Design Manual

Following the successful release of GOV.UK, the Government Digital Service committed to consolidating useful guidance into a Service Manual to pool cross-government knowledge. In a team of six people, I helped prototype the alpha, and after successful testing developed a live Beta and content guidelines that were adopted by teams throughout the civil service.

The manual today is a much-used, much-iterated tool. It still meets the vision set out by then Director of Digital Mike Bracken, providing "a shared understanding about what good looks like... ensuring that every new or redesigned service that is launched on GOV.UK is of a consistent and high quality."

Case study: Last.fm app for Spotify

A few years ago, Spotify made a brief attempt at being a platform for an ecosystem of in-player apps. With little warning, a scant tech-spec and tight dealines, I worked with a tiny team at Last.fm to produce an app that made the most of Last.fm's user-driven data with Spotify's streaming service.

Specific challenges we had to address included educated users about how Last.fm works, developing a super-streamlined sign-up flow, and delivering music discovery features that made sense in the context of Spotify's wider service.

The app proved to be one of the best-loved of Spotify's launch apps. It was deactivated when Spotify began developing its own data-driven music discovery features.
