Draft poems 26/9/23

Two poems read at OmVed gardens in Septmber 2023 (although I've edited Unfurling a bit since then).

Downdraft

Oaks sprawl, heat-heavy and tired.

They hide the sight of jays retching 'hellos' across the canopy,

And of a woodpecker,

Wheezing away to itself.


We're waiting on the downdraft

– a swoop of pressure –

To change the shape of the day.


Away from gulls (and their siren cry of chip-salt and wafer cones)

Towards wet windows,

A notebook

The static of rainfall


The first fat drop knocks a wasp from the air.

Dazed, it rests a beat on my chest,

Dashes for cover.

Then I'm away too.

Unfurling

Fox, unfurling, dappled sun drying the air.

Feet push at dirt as she stretches,

Dusty topsoil furrowing,

Falling away,

In crabapple shade.


The garden has changed.

Rain burned off, a memory.


Fox remembers spring.

Her coat, vibrant, glowed in spotlights

As the city swelled around her.


Then – before – she moved quickly,

Flitting between bright and gloom,

Confident that every space was hers.

Pressed close to vixens and dogs,

Their heat and hers melting the night.


Fox loved that spring.

Feels the lack of it.


Now fox shakes the heat off.

Pads inside.

Tiptoes into the den.

Ignores the scattered scramble left by her pups at dawn

Hours ago.


No trace of spring on her mate.

None of its lustre.

Just the leanness of this season,

And the promise of more to come.


Fox remembers spring, and longs for her coat
